ASL736

Science of Climate Change

Completed
Offered Jan 2024 – May 2026 Credits 3–0–0 CAS, IIT Delhi

Description of the climate system (general circulation, hydrological cycle, carbon cycle). The natural greenhouse effect and the effect of trace gases and aerosols. Forcings (natural and anthropogenic), fast and slow feedbacks, equilibrium climate sensitivity, and transient climate response. Climates of the past (ice ages, proxy records, abrupt climate change, the instrumental record of climate). Climate variability and time-scales — MJO, ENSO, PDO, and Milankovitch cycles. Modelling climate: simple energy-balance models and coupled climate models. Natural and anthropogenic climate change, and future climate projections.

General circulation Carbon cycle Greenhouse effect Climate feedbacks Climate sensitivity Palaeoclimate MJO · ENSO · PDO Milankovitch cycles Climate models Future projections
ASL762

Air–Sea Interaction

Ongoing
Offered July 2026 – Ongoing Credits 3–0–0 CAS, IIT Delhi

State of matter near the air–sea interface, the marine boundary layer, and transfer properties between atmosphere and ocean. Solar and terrestrial radiation, the sea-surface radiation budget, and surface wind waves. Air–sea interaction processes illustrated through ENSO, hurricanes, and the Indian monsoon. Turbulent transfer near the interface, bubbles and spray, and the transport of trace gases across the interface. Latent, sensible, and momentum fluxes in the surface boundary layer over the sea, bulk parameterizations, large-scale forcing by sea-surface buoyancy fluxes, and the spatio-temporal variability of ocean-surface fluxes with reference to the Indian Ocean.
